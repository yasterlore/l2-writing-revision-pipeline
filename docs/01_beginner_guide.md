# Beginner Guide

This repository studies how learners write, pause, revise, and develop text during L2 English free-writing tasks.

Instead of looking only at a final essay, the project treats writing as a time-ordered process. Raw browser events are eventually replayed into text states, transformed into revision events, grouped into micro-episodes, and analyzed with models that must avoid looking into the future.

## Why Keystroke-Level Data?

Keystroke-level data can show when a learner pauses, deletes, inserts, replaces, or reorganizes text. These process signals may reveal strategies that are invisible in the final text alone.

## Why Synthetic Data First?

Writing process data can be sensitive. Synthetic data lets the project build validation, tests, and reproducible workflows without exposing real participant information.
